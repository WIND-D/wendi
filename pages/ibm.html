This project is an analysis of Jira & Salesforce support tickets for Product X. The timeline of the tickets starts from 2016 Jun to now. Implementing quantitative research on knowing what and followed qualitative research on learning how. This project is dedicating to help stakeholders make data-informed design decisions from a broader sight. What can we know from the ticket analysis?

What feature buckets do we have?

By generally clustering the tickets, they are leaning to several center points which we group tickets have short distance with those points and name the groups as feature buckets.
The process:

  • Based on words popped up from “Bag of words” algorithm
  • Group similar words into feature bucket
  • Categorize ~100 tickets into feature buckets as the training sample
  • Categorize the rest ticket into feature buckets with Watson studio’s Natural language classifier service

What are the usability issues each feature bucket reflects?
How is the heuristics' distribution for each feature bucket?
How is the distribution over time?

The usability issue each ticket reflects can categorize the ticket into one of 10 Heuristics, which provides an all-around evaluation of user experience. Each ticket also has status showing if it is an archived, a working on or a fresh new ticket, telling what has been done in the past and what needs to be done now. Putting tickets into the crossing of 2 dimensions: Heuristics, ticket status, showing for Each feature bucket:
  • which feature/changes has the team made to address usability issues?
  • what features/changes should the team consider to work on?
